I'm going into college this fall with basic Python and Linux knowledge. 
That's it. No impressive background, no prior security experience.

But I've been paying attention to what's happening with LLMs — the 
jailbreaks, the prompt injections, the ways these systems fail in 
production — and I think the security side of AI is genuinely 
underexplored. Most people building these apps aren't thinking about 
how they break.

I want to be someone who does.

For the next 8 weeks I'm treating this like a full-time job. Building 
tools, documenting exploits, writing about everything I learn. By the 
time college starts I want a portfolio that actually means something.

Long term I want to work on AI safety and security at a serious company. 
That's the goal. This blog is how I get there.

Day 1 starts now.
